Nothing is more frustrating than waiting hours for a spare part only to discover it doesn't fit. Whether you're a mechanic or a car owner, getting the right part the first time saves you time, money, and headaches.

Here are five practical tips that our team and runners use every day.

## 1. Always Know Your Vehicle Spec

The same car model can come in different specs depending on where it was manufactured. A 2015 Toyota Camry built for the American market has different components than one built for the European or Middle Eastern market.

When ordering parts, always specify:

- **Make** (e.g., Toyota)
- **Model** (e.g., Camry)
- **Year** (e.g., 2015)
- **Spec** (e.g., American, European, or Nigerian-assembled)

This single step eliminates the most common cause of wrong parts.

## 2. Use OEM Part Numbers When Available

OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) part numbers are the most reliable way to identify a part. They're usually printed on the part itself or listed in the vehicle's service manual.

For example, a brake pad might have an OEM code like `04465-33471`. Giving this number to a vendor — or to us — guarantees you get an exact match.

> Pro tip: Take a photo of the old part's label before you remove it. That label often has the OEM code printed on it.

## 3. Take Photos of the Old Part

A picture is worth a thousand words, especially in the spare parts business. Before you remove a part, take clear photos showing:

- The **full part** from multiple angles
- Any **part numbers or labels** visible
- The **connector or mounting points**
- Any **damage or wear** patterns

When you order through PartsDey, our runners use your photos to verify they're picking up the exact same part.

## 4. Don't Assume All Aftermarket Parts Are Equal

Aftermarket parts range from excellent OEM-equivalent alternatives to low-quality knockoffs. Price alone doesn't tell you which is which.

Things to watch for:

- **Packaging quality** — reputable brands have consistent, professional packaging
- **Weight** — genuine parts have a specific weight. Noticeably lighter parts may use inferior materials
- **Finish quality** — look for even coatings, clean edges, and proper markings

Our runners are trained to spot quality differences and we only work with vendors who consistently provide reliable parts.

## 5. When in Doubt, Ask

If you're not sure about a part — whether it's the right fit, the right spec, or the right quality — ask before buying. It's always cheaper to clarify upfront than to return a wrong part later.
