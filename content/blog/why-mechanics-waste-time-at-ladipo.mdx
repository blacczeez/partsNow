If you run a mechanic workshop in Lagos, you already know the drill. A customer drops off their car, you diagnose the problem, and then — the waiting begins.

## The Hidden Cost of Market Trips

Every mechanic workshop in Lagos sends someone to Ladipo or ASPAMDA at least once a day. Usually it's an apprentice. Sometimes it's the mechanic themselves.

The round trip takes 2 to 4 hours depending on traffic, how quickly you find the part, and whether the first vendor has stock. That's 2 to 4 hours where:

- **The car sits idle** in your workshop, taking up space
- **The customer gets impatient** and starts calling
- **You can't start other jobs** because you're waiting on parts
- **Your apprentice isn't learning** — they're stuck in traffic

## The Numbers Don't Lie

Let's say you charge an average of ₦15,000 per job and complete 3 jobs a day. If market trips delay just one job per day, you're losing ₦15,000 daily — that's over ₦300,000 per month in potential revenue.

And that's a conservative estimate. Many workshops lose even more because delayed parts cascade into delayed appointments for the rest of the week.

## The Wrong Part Problem

Even when your apprentice makes it to the market and back quickly, there's another risk: they bring back the wrong part. Wrong spec, wrong model year, or a counterfeit that looks right but fails after a week.

Now you've lost even more time sending them back, and your customer's trust is eroding.
